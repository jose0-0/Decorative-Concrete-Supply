December has a way of making you reflective. The year winds down, the pace slows a little, and you find yourself thinking about what happened, who made it possible, and what you want to do differently or better in the year ahead.

I'm Jose Argueta. I've been running Decorative Concrete Supply in Las Vegas since before a lot of the contractors we work with were doing this work. Over 30 years in this business means I've seen a lot of years come and go and I've learned that the years worth remembering have less to do with market conditions and more to do with the people.

This is that kind of reflection.

## What 2026 Was

2026 was a year of steady, honest work. Not dramatic — no extreme market swings, no crisis to navigate, no once-in-a-generation disruption. Just consistent demand from contractors and homeowners who needed good materials, good advice, and a supplier they could count on to do what they said they'd do.

That kind of year is actually the best kind for a supply business. It's the year where the fundamentals matter most — are we stocked with what people need, are we giving accurate product guidance, are we showing up the way our customers expect us to? When the market is stable, your reputation is what's being tested.

I think we passed that test in 2026. I say that not to congratulate ourselves but because our customers kept coming back, kept referring their colleagues and their friends, and kept telling us that the relationship matters to them. That's the test that counts.

## The Contractors We Work With

The contractors who buy from DCS aren't just customers to us. They're the people who take the materials we sell and turn them into something a homeowner is proud of. They're the ones who show up early on hot summer mornings in Las Vegas because the concrete is coming and the clock is running. They're the ones who've invested in learning their craft and who take real pride in work that holds up and looks good years later.

In 2026 we worked with contractors who were new to decorative work and were figuring it out for the first time. We worked with contractors who have been doing this for twenty years and were trying a new product system. We worked with contractors who called us from a job site with a problem and needed an answer fast.

Every one of those conversations matters. The new contractor who gets the right guidance on their first overlay project goes on to do more overlay projects. The experienced contractor who tries a new sealer system and has it work well is more confident in their next recommendation to a customer. The contractor who calls from the job site and gets a real answer instead of a voicemail stays the kind of contractor who calls us.

We're grateful for every one of those relationships.

## The Homeowners Who Trusted Us

Some of our best conversations in 2026 were with homeowners who had never set foot in a concrete supply house before. They found us through a search or a referral and walked in not entirely sure what they needed.

What they got was a conversation. Someone who listened to what they were trying to do, looked at the photos they brought in, asked a few questions about the surface and the conditions and the result they were after, and gave them a straight answer about what would work and what wouldn't. Sometimes that answer was that what they wanted to do was a reasonable DIY project. Sometimes it was that the project was more involved than they realized and they'd be better served by a contractor. Either answer was the honest one.

Homeowners who trust a supply house with those conversations are trusting us with their money and their project. That trust isn't something we take lightly and it's something we try to earn every time.

## What We're Carrying Into 2026

There are a few things I want to make sure we do well in 2026 that I've been thinking about as the year closes.

I want to keep improving the knowledge our team brings to customer conversations. The decorative concrete product landscape keeps evolving — new formulations, new product categories, new application techniques. Staying current so we can give relevant, accurate guidance is part of the job and it requires consistent effort.

I want to deepen the relationships with the contractors who count on us. The contractors who have been buying from us for ten, fifteen, twenty years have built their businesses in part on the consistency we provide. Continuing to earn that trust year after year is not something that happens automatically — it requires showing up the same way every day.

I want to continue being honest with customers even when honesty isn't the most comfortable answer. Telling someone their surface needs replacement rather than overlay when that's the right call. Telling a contractor that a product they want to use isn't the right fit for their application. Being the kind of resource that people can trust to give them accurate information rather than the answer they want to hear.

## A Word on This Blog

Starting in January 2026 we launched a series of articles on the DCS website covering topics that matter to contractors and homeowners working with decorative concrete in Las Vegas. Over the course of the year we published 48 articles covering everything from how to read a spec sheet to how to bid a decorative concrete job to why local supply matters more than online ordering.

The goal was always to give real, useful information rather than marketing content dressed up as education. Whether we succeeded at that is for our readers to judge. What I can say is that every article was written with the intention of being genuinely helpful — the kind of information I'd want to have if I were a contractor or homeowner trying to make a good decision about a concrete project.

Thank you to everyone who read these articles, shared them with colleagues, or came into one of our locations because something they read pointed them in our direction.

## Thank You

Running a business for over 30 years in a market like Las Vegas means surviving the ups and downs that come with a city that has seen dramatic growth and dramatic contraction. Through all of it, what has kept DCS here is customers who believed in what we were doing and gave us the business that made it possible.

To every contractor who drove to our South Las Vegas or North Las Vegas location this year, who called us with a question, who recommended us to someone they knew — thank you. That loyalty is the foundation of this business and it's not something I ever take for granted.

To every homeowner who trusted us with their project, however big or small — thank you for choosing to work with a local business that's invested in this community.
