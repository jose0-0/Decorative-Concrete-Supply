## A Brief Background

Solomon Colors was founded in 1928 and has been producing concrete pigments and color systems ever since. Nearly a century of focus on a single product category produces a depth of product knowledge and formulation expertise that generalist manufacturers can't match.

The company manufactures iron oxide pigments — the core colorants used in integral concrete coloring, color hardener, and various concrete tinting applications. Iron oxide pigments are the industry standard for concrete coloring because they're UV-stable, chemically inert in the alkaline concrete environment, and durable over the long service life of concrete structures. Solomon Colors is one of the major manufacturers of these pigments in the United States.

That manufacturing background matters because it means Solomon is not just a distributor of pigments made elsewhere. They control the product from production through distribution, which gives them consistency and quality control that resellers of commodity pigments can't always guarantee.

## What Solomon Colors Actually Makes

The product line is broader than many contractors realize. Most people know Solomon for integral color — the liquid or powder pigments added to the concrete mix to produce colored concrete throughout the entire slab. But the full product range covers several categories relevant to decorative concrete work.

**Integral colors** are the core of the line. Available in liquid and granular form, these pigments are added to ready-mix concrete at the batch plant or on the job site to produce consistent color throughout the slab. The liquid form disperses easily and produces uniform color distribution. The granular form is convenient for on-site addition when exact batching equipment isn't available.

The color palette is extensive. Solomon offers standard colors across the spectrum of earth tones, grays, and other popular concrete colors, along with the ability to custom-match colors for specific project requirements. For contractors doing work that needs to match existing concrete or meet an architect's color specification, the custom matching capability is a real asset.

**Color hardener** is another significant product category. Solomon's color hardener line is used extensively in stamped concrete work to add color and surface density to fresh concrete before stamping. The consistency of color from bag to bag matters enormously in stamped work because variation between batches shows in the finished surface. Solomon's manufacturing consistency is part of why their color hardener is trusted for projects where color uniformity is critical.

**Release agents** used with stamped concrete to prevent stamp mats from adhering to the surface and to add the secondary accent color that gives stamped work its depth and character. The release color interacts with the base color from the color hardener to create the layered appearance that quality stamped concrete is known for. Solomon's release agent formulations are designed to work with their color hardener products, which takes some of the guesswork out of color combination selection.

**Sealers and surface treatments** round out the line, providing the protective topcoat that locks in color and protects the finished surface. Using a sealer formulated to work with the color system below it reduces the risk of compatibility issues between products from different manufacturers.

## How Integral Color Works in Practice

For contractors newer to integrally colored concrete, a brief explanation of how the product is used in practice.

Integral color is added to the concrete mix to produce color throughout the entire slab rather than just at the surface. This means that if the surface chips or wears, the color below is the same — there's no gray concrete showing through. For flatwork that will see heavy use, integral color is more durable than surface-applied color in terms of appearance over time.

Liquid integral color is typically added at the batch plant when the ready-mix order is placed. The dosage rate determines the color intensity — higher dosage produces deeper color, lower dosage produces a lighter version of the same hue. Most ready-mix plants in Las Vegas are familiar with Solomon liquid colors and can batch to a specified dosage.

Granular integral color can be added to the truck on the job site by mixing it with water and adding it to the drum. This is practical for smaller orders or for situations where the batch plant isn't equipped to add integral color at the plant. The granular form requires sufficient additional mixing after addition to ensure even distribution throughout the load.

Color variation between loads is an inherent characteristic of integrally colored concrete and worth discussing with customers before the project begins. Even with consistent dosing, slight variation between pours from different trucks or different batching days is possible. Designing the pour sequence to minimize visible transitions between loads, and discussing realistic expectations with the customer upfront, prevents most of the disappointment that can come from this characteristic.

## Using Solomon Release Agents With Color Hardener

For stamped concrete work, the combination of Solomon color hardener and Solomon release agent produces predictable results that contractors can communicate to customers with confidence.

The base color from the color hardener establishes the dominant tone of the finished slab. The release agent color adds the secondary accent that reads on the high points of the stamp texture and creates the depth that makes quality stamped concrete look like natural stone or aged masonry rather than colored concrete.

Solomon provides combination guides that show how specific color hardener and release agent combinations look together on finished slabs. These guides are practical reference tools for design conversations with customers and for specification on jobs where the customer needs to approve the color combination before the pour.
